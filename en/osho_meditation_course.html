Osho Meditation Course

This is a very joyful, playful, still and inspiring course. You will experience the benefits of the course throughout your whole life and you will meet fellow travellers who are longing for depth, inner silence and joy in daily life.
  • 7 Tuesday evenings 19:30 - 22:00
  • 2 new Osho meditations per evening & integrating meditation into your daily life
  • at my home: Kettingstraat 22, 5611RD Eindhoven
  • €160.00 including the book 'Depression, a Stepping-Stone towards Bliss'

Peace of mind and joy in your daily life is what this course gives you. The active Osho meditations help you to first release your busy mind, after which it is easier to reach your inner silence. I give you short exercises which help you to integrate meditation into your daily life. The course is light, full of suprises and inspiration.

When I meet participants years later, they tell me enthusiastically that they still use the exercises and meditations in their daily lives and that they enrich their lives.

"Hallo, more than 20 years ago I came across Osho meditations, facilitated by Modita. I was a participant in her meditation group. It was a big step to participate in this and everything was completely new. But the evenings were exciting, beautiful and loving. Nice and like-minded people in the group and every week a new meditation. I learned, no, I started to look differently at myself and at the things around me. Left behind more and more the judgements and love and compassion came in its place. Looking forward to the evenings I went to the course every week and returned home with a smile on my face afterwards (and with the homework!).
Meditation is so diverse and sometimes unrecognizibly present, that if I would dissect my day, I would see that I apply every day something of meditation. For example the here and now exercise (consciously focusing my attention on what surrounds me to come out of my head) or slowing down my speed while walking, or leaning back and looking (observing the other or myself), having pain in my belly and giving it my attention. Enjoying music, washing dishes or hanging up the laundry with attention etc. Meditation is not just sitting still or listening to music, but so much more. By continuing the active meditations I am aware of when I should meditate and which meditation I can do best. I think and am not sure of this, but most probably I apply it unnoticed when I need it. It has become part of myself. Because of this I have become who I am now." - Anonymous
